Controls

The top bar contains several menus.

The first menu lets you pick which type of regular polytope to display.

The second menu lets you pick the dimension of the polytope.

Next is a check box showing if the slice is visible or not. When you check the "slice" checkbox, an intersection of the slice with a hyperplane will be displayed. The slide-bar at the bottom of the screen allows you to control the height of the hyperplane. See slice for more information.

After the slice checkbox is the transformation menu. Initially, it is set to "Rotate R3". If you have a keyboard, you can also use the shift, control, or alt keys to change which transformation is active.

Rotate R3

If you drag your mouse or finger, the polytope will rotate in 3 dimensions. Use two fingers or the mouse scroll wheel to scale the polytope.

Rotate R4

If you drag your mouse or finger, the polytope will rotate in 4 dimensions. That means that instead of rotating in the x-z plane and the y-z plane, the polytope will rotate in the x-w plane or y-w plane.

Rotate R5

If you drag your mouse or finger, the polytope will rotate in 5 dimensions.

Zolly

Dragging the mouse or finger will change the perspective transformation. I.e. things that are farther away will either a lot smaller or just a little smaller.

Zolly is a contraction of zoom and dolly, and I did not make up that word.

Translate

Dragging the mouse or finger will move the polytope.

Vertex First

Rotate the polytope so that a vertex is closest to the camera. A new slice intersection will hit the vertex first.

Edge First

Rotate the polytope so that an edge is closest to the camera. A new slice intersection will hit the edge first.

Face First

Rotate the polytope so that a face is closest to the camera. A new slice intersection will hit the face first.

3-Cell First

Rotate the polytope so that a 3-cell is closest to the camera. A new slice intersection will hit the 3-cell first.

Velocity

If you fling your mouse or finger while rotating in R3, the polytope will continue to rotate. Click or tap in the screen to stop it.

If you fling your mouse or finger while adjusting the slice slidebar, the slice height will continue to change. Click or tap on the slider to stop it.

Settings

On the settings screen you can change the theme color, as well as the color of the polytope and the slice.

The sticky delay and sticky radius allow the slidebar to pause for a short time at important points. An important point is where the hyperplane intersects a point, edge, or face at only one height instead of in a range.

The "hotkey buttons for touchscreen" adds buttons on a touchscreen that mimic the effects of the shift, alt, and control keys.

Leave the "Periodic Debug Logs" off. Pay no attention to the man behind the curtain.
