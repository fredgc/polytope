Slices

To help visualize an n-dimensional polytope, this program will display the intersection of the polytope with an (n-1)-dimensional hyperplane. The intersection will also be a convex polytope, but it might not be regular. When the slice is active, there will be a slider at the bottom of the screen that controls the height of the hyperplane.

To understand what is going on, it might help to start with a cube in 3 dimensions. Click the slice button to create the slice. Move the slider at the bottom of the screen back and forth and you will see that the intersection of a cube with a plane is a square.

That is, it is a square if the cube has one face parallel to the plane. Click on the rotation menu and select "Edge first". Then recreate the slice. Now you will see that the intersection starts of as an edge, turns into a rectangle that gets fatter until it is a square, and then shrinks back to an edge before disappearing.

Now rotate the cube to be "Vertex first" and recreate the slice. This time, the intersection will start off as a point and grow into a triangle. When the triangle gets as large as it can, it starts to loose its corners. At the middle of the cube, the intersection is a hexagon.

Once you feel you understand what you are looking at, try intersecting a 4-cube with a 3-dimensional hyperplane.
